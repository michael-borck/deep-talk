# Analysis

After a transcript finishes transcribing, DeepTalk sends the text to your configured AI provider and runs a suite of analyses. This page explains what each analysis means and how to read the results.

## What gets analysed

Every new transcript gets:

- **Summary** — a few paragraphs capturing what the recording is about
- **Key topics** — the main subjects discussed
- **Action items** — concrete to-dos extracted from the conversation
- **Sentiment** — overall tone (positive / negative / neutral / mixed) with a numeric score
- **Emotions** — a breakdown of detected emotions with relative strengths
- **Notable quotes** — the most striking or representative lines
- **Research themes** — recurring ideas with confidence scores and examples
- **Q&A pairs** — detected questions and their answers, with speakers and timestamps
- **Concept frequency** — how often specific concepts appear and where

You'll find them on the transcript detail page under **Overview** and **Analysis** tabs.

## The AI provider matters

Analysis quality is bounded by the LLM you're using. A 3B-parameter local model will produce decent summaries and basic themes. GPT-4-class models produce noticeably richer analysis, especially for nuanced topics like sentiment and research themes.

Check **Settings → Processing → AI Analysis Service** to pick your provider. DeepTalk supports Ollama, OpenAI, Anthropic, Groq, Gemini, OpenRouter, and any OpenAI-compatible custom endpoint.

Switching providers doesn't retroactively re-run analysis on existing transcripts — re-analyse from the transcript detail page if you want to refresh.

## Overview tab

The landing view for a transcript. Everything here is a glance-level readout:

- **Summary** at the top, with a word count and generated-on timestamp
- **Key topics** as chips
- **Action items** as a checklist (you can tick them off; state is saved)
- **Sentiment** card with overall tone and a numeric score
- **Emotions** card with a bar chart
- **Notable quotes** with speaker attribution and a jump-to-timestamp link

## Analysis tab

Deeper views:

### Research themes

Each theme is shown with:

- The **theme label** (generated by the AI)
- A **confidence score** (0-1)
- **Example quotes** drawn from the transcript

Useful for qualitative research and interview coding. The AI looks for recurring conceptual patterns, not just repeated words.

### Q&A pairs

When the transcript is a conversation or interview, DeepTalk extracts explicit question-answer pairs. Each pair shows:

- The **question** with speaker and timestamp
- The **answer** with speaker and timestamp
- A jump link into the transcript

Good for structured interviews where you want to revisit specific exchanges.

### Concept frequency

A word/phrase frequency table that counts meaningful concepts (not stop words). Each row shows:

- The **concept**
- Its **count** across the transcript
- **Contexts** — surrounding snippets showing where it appears

Useful for quickly locating mentions of a specific idea.

### Talk-time per speaker

A horizontal bar showing total talk-time for each speaker. Speaker labels come from diarisation; you can rename them in Speaker Tagging if they're not meaningful yet.

### Filler words

Counts of common hesitation markers ("um", "uh", "like", "you know"). Useful for presentation coaching or self-review.

### Conversation quality

Higher-level metrics:

- **Turn-taking balance** — how evenly speakers shared the floor
- **Question-to-statement ratio** — how interrogative the conversation was
- **Average turn length**
- **Interruption count**

## Re-running analysis

Click **Re-analyse** at the top of the transcript detail page to regenerate everything with the currently configured AI provider. Useful after:

- Switching from a small local model to a cloud model
- Customising prompts in **Settings → AI Prompts**
- Correcting the transcript (analysis runs on the corrected text by default — see the setting in Processing)

## Project-level analysis

If a transcript belongs to a project, the project also runs its own analysis across the combined text of every member. That's covered in the [Projects guide](../user-guide/projects.md).

## Customising what the AI does

**Settings → AI Prompts** lets you edit the exact prompt used for each analysis task. Each prompt has a default you can restore if you break something. Variables like `{transcript}` are filled in at runtime.

Use this to bias the AI toward specific aspects ("always look for action items related to deadlines") or to adapt the analysis to your domain (research coding, sales calls, lecture notes).

## Token usage

Each analysis call consumes tokens on your AI provider. DeepTalk tracks session-level usage in **Settings → Processing → Session token usage** so you can see roughly how much you're spending on hosted providers. Totals reset when the app restarts.
